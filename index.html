About

I am a student at Brigham Young University studying computer science. I've also completed coursework in finance, accounting, and business management. I will graduate in April 2019.

My greatest passion is music. Music has been a part of my life since I was 5, when I started with classical piano. As a teenager, I moved to composing film score and taking lessons during the summer at a recording studio. I was accepted to BYU's Commercial Music program, but I dropped it after my mission because I wasn't getting the kind of musical education that I wanted. I now play piano and synthesizer for Suit Up, Soldier, a rock band in Provo that I joined in March 2017.

After I dropped the music program, I studied finance. I worked at Utah Community Credit Union as a loan officer for over a year, and I interned at Zion's Bank summer of 2017 in their small business banking group. I didn't like the sales aspect or the culture of most finance jobs, so I switched to computer science two months ago. Following are reasons I switched to computer science:

  1. More creative job roles
  2. Better job market
  3. Skills that are applicable to my own personal projects
